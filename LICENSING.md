# Workproba Licensing

Workproba is distributed under a **dual licensing** model.

| Use case | License | Fee |
|----------|---------|-----|
| Personal, non-professional use | [WPEL](LICENSE) | Free |
| Individual students, instructors, academic research | [WPEL](LICENSE) | Free |
| Companies, institutions, production, internal IT | [Commercial](LICENSE-COMMERCIAL) | Paid |
| Evaluation by an organization (30 days) | [WPEL](LICENSE) | Free |

## Quick decision guide

**Free under WPEL**

- You are an individual using Workproba for yourself, not for an employer or client.
- You are a student or teacher using it for coursework, teaching, or non-commercial academic research.
- Your organization is evaluating Workproba for up to 30 days (non-production).

**Commercial license required**

- Any use by or on behalf of a company, government agency, NGO, hospital, or school **as an institution** (IT, admin, staff tooling, campus-wide deployment).
- Production use, paid services, SaaS, or client work.
- Use by employees, contractors, or consultants in a professional context.

## FAQ

### Can a university use Workproba for free?

**Students and faculty**, for individual coursework, teaching, or academic research: yes, under WPEL.

**The university as an organization** (central IT, lab infrastructure, administrative use, campus-wide rollout): no — a commercial license is required.

### Can I use Workproba at work on personal projects?

If you are using employer equipment, networks, or acting in your professional role, that generally requires a commercial license — even if the project is not customer-facing.

### Can I fork and contribute?

Yes. Contributions you submit may be used by Improba under WPEL, commercial terms, or otherwise, unless a separate contributor agreement applies.

### Is WPEL an OSI-approved open source license?

No. WPEL is a **source-available** community license with commercial restrictions. It is not submitted to the Open Source Initiative.
